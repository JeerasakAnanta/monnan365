# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: hackathon judges and organizers of "Nan Beyond Seasons Challenge 2026" (Track 1: AI Trip Planner & Concierge), viewing this as a pitch deck (`docs/slide-pitch.html`) during a live judged presentation — likely on a projector, limited time per team (~2nd of 7 teams presenting). Secondary: anyone reviewing the project afterward via the deck link.

Job to be done: quickly understand the problem, solution, AI/data mechanism, and impact of MonNan365 (มนต์น่าน 365) well enough to score it against judging criteria (impact, community understanding, creativity, AI use, bonus).

## Product Purpose

MonNan365 is an AI trip planner that spreads tourism in Nan province, Thailand across all 12 months instead of concentrating it in the winter high season, by steering travelers toward secondary attractions and community enterprises (≥35% quota enforced in planning logic). This deck (`docs/slide-pitch.html`) is the pitch presentation used to explain that product to hackathon judges — a self-contained, single-file HTML slide deck with keyboard/touch navigation, speaker notes, and print-to-PDF support.

## Brand Personality

Nature / editorial, calm, elevated. Rooted in Nan's forest-green and gold identity (existing palette: `--nan-forest`, `--nan-leaf`, `--nan-gold`, `--nan-earth`, `--nan-cream`). Should read as considered and trustworthy — a serious civic/tourism proposal — not a flashy startup pitch. Calm confidence over hype.

## Anti-references

- Not a bold/energetic neon startup-pitch look.
- Not generic flat "SaaS cliché" slide templates (gradient text, hero-metric blocks, side-stripe cards) — these are explicitly banned by the design system in use.
- Not overly minimal/sparse either — the deck should feel warm and grounded in Nan's natural identity, not clinical.

## Design Principles

1. Every slide must stay legible from the back of a room on a projector — contrast and type scale take priority over decoration.
2. Reinforce the Nan forest/gold/earth identity already established in the existing palette; extend it, don't replace it.
3. Backgrounds and texture should evoke Nan's nature (mist, forest, terraced hills) without competing with foreground text.
4. Judges skim fast — visual hierarchy (headline → key stat/claim → supporting detail) must be obvious at a glance per slide.
5. Print/PDF export (`@media print`) must keep working; don't rely on effects that vanish or break when printed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No special accommodation beyond standard good practice. Maintain readable contrast (aim for WCAG AA, ≥4.5:1 body text) since the deck may be viewed on a dim projector.
